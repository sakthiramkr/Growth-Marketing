The G2 review correction loop
▼
When Perplexity described TC's Starter plan as missing email workflows, the fix was to trace which source the model was pulling from, update the outdated G2 review, and re-run the monitoring prompts. Output corrected over the following weeks. Fix the source, not the model.
📈
Brand sentiment tracking
▼
Monitored sentiment signals across G2, Capterra, and Reddit. Not just whether TC appeared in AI outputs, but how it was described. Positive, specific review content directly influences the framing AI systems use when generating comparison responses.
Built from scratch

Competitive
intelligence engine

A Claude API workflow that aggregated competitor signals, classified urgency, and surfaced one pattern that moved a product roadmap.

What it was

Competitor pages monitored via VisualPing for changes. Signals from G2, Capterra, Reddit, and CRM win/loss data across 4 rivals. The Claude API classified each signal by urgency, theme, and which team needed to act.

What it produced

Weekly CI newsletter with separate action items per team: Marketing, Sales, and Product. A hypothesis engine that triggered when 3+ signals formed a pattern. Battlecard update flags when competitor pricing or features changed.

⚡ Impact

The system flagged SCORM compliance as a recurring theme across competitor signals and win/loss data. Customers were asking about it, competitors were promoting it, deals were stalling. Surfaced this pattern to the product team. It got prioritised on the roadmap and restored velocity on stalled enterprise deals.

Why Revolut

Revolut operates at a scale where a 1% shift in how AI systems describe the brand has measurable downstream impact on acquisition. When 75 million users research financial products, a growing share of them start with ChatGPT or Perplexity. What those systems say, whether it's accurate, favourable, and ahead of competitors, is a real commercial problem. That's the problem I want to work on.

The LMS and fintech evaluation dynamics are closer than they look. Both are high-consideration purchases where buyers research heavily before deciding. The GEO levers are the same: structured data, third-party citation management, entity coverage, prompt monitoring. The scale is different. The approach is not.
